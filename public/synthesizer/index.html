Searching

Search for possible pathways to a chemical by entering above its standard InChI, a common name, or its Act ID.
For example, for tylenol, you can enter any one of the following:

  1. InChI=1S/C8H9NO2/c1-6(10)9-7-2-4-8(11)5-3-7/h2-5,11H,1H3,(H,9,10)
  2. paracetamol
  3. 12832


Interpreting the result

Chemical nodes are pale green except for the target node which is bright green. Reaction nodes are white labeled by the number of reaction entries in our database it represents. Green edges show the required reactants; black edges point to its products. Common cofactors are labeled on the edges, with different possibilities separated by parentheses. You can mouseover the nodes for information about the chemicals and reactions.


Note that the graphs shown may not be complete, displaying all possible pathways from our defined set of native metabolites) as there is a limit of about 50 chemicals shown in to avoid clutter. A red outline denotes that some paths to the chemical aren't displayed. A green outline denotes a member of our initial set of metabolites. Only concretely observed reactions are used.
